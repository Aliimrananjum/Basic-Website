Topical Information

Data security and privacy

hoose a social media that you use, and try to find out what kind of information this company has about you, and whether they share information about you to third parties. Is it easy to find and understand information about privacy in on the platform you have chosen?

endre

We have always been aware that big tech companies were using our data to “better understand our needs” and make a “better and more personalized experience” for their users, amongst other excuses, however what we were not aware of is how much data and the type of data they gather. Bizarre metrics about us we never knew we shared with the Web.

endre

One look at Instagram’s official Data Policy web page (Instagram, 2021) and you’ll feel overwhelmed with all the different ways your data is stored and why they chose to archive said data. For example; they store different metrics about your device's camera, system storage space and devices connected to the same network as you. Things that you would probably never share on your own if asked to.

endre

So the information about data and privacy is available to you, if you look for it that is. What you’re actually agreeing to share when signing up for Instagram isn’t made obvious for you. You won’t get a pop-up saying: “You’re about to share your love for SHEEP in IRELAND with a whole lot of third party companies, are you sure you want this?” (BBC News, 2018) Of course there is the possibility that this actually is presented to you, in the infamous “terms and conditions,” that no one actually reads.

endre

Jokes aside, this could be a good place to begin. Make these terms more available and easily accessible to the users. We shouldn’t have to dig for the information or wait for a big scandal to expose them. Visualize clearly why Instagram (Facebook) wants this particular data from us and what it will be used for. They’re getting rich on our data, we might as well have access to it ourselves.
